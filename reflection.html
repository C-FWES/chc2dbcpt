Is Canada a country to be proud of?

Canada thrives in its fair share of shameful historical moments. For example, the conscription debate bitterly divided English and French Canadians and formed a disconnect that continues today. The forced internment of Japanese Canadians shed light upon the paranoia and anti-Asian sentiment that existed in Canada. The cancellation of the groundbreaking Avro Arrow destroyed a nation’s hopes and dreams to become a leading aerial power, and the October Crisis bore the limits of how far the government could restrict civil liberties. However, just as it is with anything else, failure is necessary for a nation to grow. These events and mistakes are ingrained in history for generations to view and learn, in order not to relive the demise that they had brought. In a way, these have even led to the betterment of Canada. For example, Canada is now known worldwide for being an accommodating and inclusive nation that accepts all people. The divide between French and English is closing due to increased education about French culture and respect for multiculturalism. Furthermore, Canada’s aviation capabilities are growing, as the nation now boasts the fifth largest aviation industry in the world. As a nation that had its downfalls but recovered, Canada is a nation to be proud of.
